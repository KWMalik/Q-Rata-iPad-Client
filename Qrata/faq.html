Ratings, Rankings and Recommendations

How do you select a site, app or video to include in QRata?

 Actually, QRata itself does not select any content. Rather, our experts and user make the selections. Our experts are knowledgeable in their subject area and spend much of their professional life scouring the Internet to find the most reliable and useful content in that subject. Our experts start their selection process by screening for content that warrants further evaluation as potentially superior and rates it for a ranking. QRata constantly seeks to upgrade our content with user suggestions to ensure our database is unsurpassed in delivering what you need-to-know when you need to know it. Users earn a cash reward for identifying superior content experts missed (click here to learn more).

How is information content for a site, app or video rated?

 QRata ratings are the result of a rigorous, patented evaluation process. It starts with a basic criteria scorecard for evaluating a site, app or videoâ€™s operation, functionality, accuracy, and so on. Next, experts weight each of the criteria to reflect its importance in the overall evaluation of the topic. Then experts use the criteria to score the actual content offered. An average score for all the criteria is then calculated to create an overall rating for each content resource, which is then ranked against all content in the specific topic area. The result is rank order of the most reliable sites, apps and videos recommended by QRata. In addition to user challenges, our team of editors and quality control people audit this process continuously to ensure you obtain only superior content results.

What are the basic rating criteria?

 At a minimum, criteria include scope, coverage, accuracy, consistency, currency, richness, completeness, size, navigation, searching and browsing, internal and external links, output features, speed, availability, and privacy (click here to learn more).

Is the difference between the higher rated or ranked recommendations significant?

 First, and most important, QRata ONLY list on average the top 5 - 10 sites, apps or videos in a topic area. In other words, we only offer you the best-of-the-best content. So, while there is always some notable difference between content listed within a given topic, a lower rating - thus a lower ranking - does not imply the content resource is a less worthy of consideration. Rather, a higher rating, and thus ranking, often implies a site, app or video has more of the elements, features and attributes you would want if you had to choose just one site. Since all QRata recommendations undergo the same evaluation process, most of the time the lower rated and ranked content offers something unique or different that may be more specific or relevant to what you are seeking. In other words, the content may have some strength or feature that makes it valuable or special in comparison to other top-rated content within a given topic area. This unique or specialize aspect is usually identified in the expertâ€™s content summary adjacent to the rating score and can be quite helpful.

Why would a great site, app or video I know get a low rating (or not be included) in QRata?

 It is hard to say in the abstract. QRata experts screen and evaluate many aspects of many content resources before they recommend their top resources picks. Note, however, with our patented evaluation process we do not ask experts to directly compare and evaluate one content resource against another. Rather, we have them compare each resource against the highest standard that can be set in each topic area. Consequently, any particular resource may excel in some criteria and be deficient in others. Since we limit the number of top-rated and recommended resources listed in a given topic, many otherwise good or average resources do not make it into our database. Key to the real benefit of our rating approach is that it both reduces any unconscious bias an expert may have and assures you they are objectively rating and recommending only the most reliable content possible.
